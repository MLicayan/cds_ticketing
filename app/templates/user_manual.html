CDS Service Monitoring - User Manual

Operational guide for tickets, tasks, service logs, schedules, monitoring, instruments, reports, and administration.

Overview

CDS Service Monitoring tracks service tickets, developer/IT tasks, service logs, preventive maintenance schedules, weekly schedules, instruments, application monitoring, and reports.

Access is role-based. Administrators can also customize module access per user through navigation permissions.

Roles & Permissions
Admin
Full access to operational modules, reports, monitoring, and Administrator setup screens.
Engineer / Support
Handles tickets, service logs, schedules, instruments, exports, and reports when granted.
IT
Usually works from My Task or Developer Tasks, plus tickets/service logs when granted.
Sales
Creates and follows tickets for assigned clients; protected ticket fields and service-log creation are restricted.
Client
Creates and follows own tickets, views own instruments, and approves closures where required.
Client Admin
Views tickets for the assigned client organization with client-scoped access.
Main Navigation
Dashboard: role-scoped KPIs, summaries, workload snapshots, and schedule/calendar context.
All Tickets / Tickets: filters, details, comments, attachments, Gantt, calendar, kanban, and CSV export.
My Task / My Tickets: assigned work for staff or client-specific ticket access, depending on role.
Developer Tasks: IT/developer child tasks with filters and task details.
App Monitoring / Daily Monitoring: application and daily status views for permitted users.
Service Logs: service records, parts, signatures, CSV export, and PDF generation.
Weekly / PM Schedule: planned work, PM visits, comments, attachments, and linked logs.
Instruments: inventory, service history, QR links, and LIS status information.
Reports: top instruments, visits/defects, and engineer activity.
Profile: account details, password changes, and client-user management where enabled.
Administrator: setup for users, clients, instruments, models, parts, apps, and service types.
Ticket Workflow
  1. Create: go to Tickets -> New Ticket, choose the client and whether the ticket is for an instrument or CDS application, then fill subject, description, priority/date details, and optional attachment/photo. Client users are automatically scoped to their own client. Ticket numbers use T-000001 format.
  2. Review/update: ticket detail shows status, priority, assignee, target/date-needed fields, timeline, comments, attachments, related tasks, and linked service logs. Admin/Engineer users can update protected fields when permitted.
  3. Comments: public comments support normal collaboration. Staff can mark notes as internal. Replies keep discussion threaded, and reactions/acknowledgements show that a comment was seen or accepted.
  4. Notifications: new client comments can appear in the header notification bell for staff.
  5. Progress views: use list filters, Gantt, Calendar, and Kanban. Permitted staff can drag non-closed tickets between Kanban columns.
  6. Closure: client-reported tickets require a captured client signature before closing. Closing a parent ticket also closes its child tasks.
Developer / IT Tasks
  • Developer Tasks are child work items connected to a parent ticket.
  • Each task has its own task number, assignee, status, priority, comments, replies, reactions, attachments, and working/not-working state.
  • Use the Developer Tasks page to search and filter by client, assigned IT user, priority, status, and date range.
  • Closed tasks are read-only.
Service Log Workflow
  • Create: go to Service Logs -> New, or launch from a ticket/PM schedule to prefill fields.
  • Required details: client, instrument or application, engineer, service type, visit date, start/end time, status after service, confirmation photo, confirmed by, and confirmed by position.
  • Signatures: onsite/non-remote service types require a customer signature; remote service types may skip it depending on setup.
  • Parts: add parts with quantity, price, total, and warranty flag as needed.
  • Monitoring: mark logs for monitoring and set monitored days where applicable.
  • Ticket sync: operational logs move linked tickets to Fix/Completed/Resolved; non-operational logs keep or return them to In-Process if not closed.
  • Exports: CSV from the Service Logs list and PDF from the log detail page.
Preventive Maintenance Schedule
  • Create PM work from PM Schedule -> New with client, instrument, date, description, task duration, and assigned engineer.
  • PM numbers are generated automatically.
  • Use filters by client, instrument, and date range to find planned work.
  • Open PM detail to add comments, upload attachments, assign/reassign work, edit the schedule, or create a linked service log when completed.
Weekly Schedule
  • Create a weekly plan from Weekly Schedule -> New.
  • Set the week start and title, then add task rows for client, instrument/application, service type, subject, notes, engineer, priority, and optional PM schedule.
  • Weekly task rows help drive planned work and can link back to tickets/PM schedules where applicable.
Instruments
  • Instruments are scoped by client access.
  • The list shows inventory details such as client, model, serial number, installation/warranty dates, status, and LIS connection information.
  • Instrument detail pages show service history, linked tickets/logs, last maintenance/calibration context, and QR access.
Application & Daily Monitoring
  • App Monitoring summarizes current CDS application/integration status.
  • Daily Monitoring provides a day-by-day monitoring view.
  • Monitoring pages are available only when the user has the matching navigation permission.
Reports
  • Reports are available to Admin and permitted Engineer users.
  • Report data includes top instruments by visits/defects and engineer activity/log counts.
  • Client-scoped users only see data allowed by their client assignment.
Profile
  • Use Profile to update account details and password.
  • Client Admin users may manage users under their assigned client when enabled.
Administrator Setup
  • System Setup: manage navigation permissions available to users.
  • Users: manage Admins, Engineers/IT, Sales, and client users.
  • Clients: maintain client records, contacts, assigned sales, status, mapped CDS applications, and client users.
  • Instruments and Models: maintain instruments, client links, model code, brand, and machine type.
  • Parts, CDS Application, Service Types: maintain operational reference data.
Attachments, Signatures & Exports
  • Tickets, ticket tasks, service logs, and PM schedules support attachments.
  • Ticket closure signatures are stored as ticket attachments.
  • Service log customer signatures are stored as service log attachments, and confirmation photos are required for service log creation.
  • Tickets export to CSV from the Tickets page. Service Logs export to CSV from the list and PDF from log detail.
Troubleshooting
  • Cannot see a module: ask an admin to check role and navigation permissions.
  • Cannot close a client-reported ticket: capture and submit the closure signature in the modal.
  • Cannot edit a ticket/task: closed items and protected fields may be read-only for your role.
  • Service log save is blocked: check client, target instrument/application, engineer, service type, visit date, confirmation photo, confirmed by details, and required signature.
  • Missing service time in PDF: edit the service log and fill Start Time and End Time.
  • Client sees too little or too much data: verify the user's client assignment and whether the account is Client or Client Admin.
